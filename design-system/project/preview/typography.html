Playfair Display

The headline voice — sparingly.

Playfair is reserved for the top of each section and one standout phrase per page. It sets tone, but it doesn't do the reading. Keep it at 28px and up, always at heavier weights (700+), and always paired with generous line-height so the italic descenders don't crash into the line above.

Four rules that keep type honest
1

Playfair is a guest, not the host.

Limit Playfair to the page's big statement and one or two section heads. Poppins carries everything else — never run Playfair as body copy.

2

One italic per headline.

Italic Playfair is an emphasis, not a style. One phrase per headline, always in brand blue, and never a full sentence.

3

Watch the descenders.

At display size, italic f, j, and p reach below the baseline. Use line-height 1.15 minimum, and add 0.06em of padding-left on italic spans to keep them off the preceding character.

4

Sentence case, not all-caps.

Eyebrows, labels, and nav items stay in natural case. If a label needs to shout, make it larger or bolder — don't capitalize it.

Ag

Minion stays with the print shop.

Minion Pro remains the heritage mark — it lives on the certificate of warranty, packaging, business cards, and the wordmark lock-up. Keep it out of screens: it's designed for ink on paper, and its tight spacing produces the fi and fl collisions you see on display monitors. Playfair Display is the screen voice.
